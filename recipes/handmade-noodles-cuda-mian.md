# Handmade Noodles (CUDA Mian)

A guide to making handmade noodles, with two methods: autolyse and rolling cut. Includes instructions for a classic chili oil topping.

![Handmade Noodles (CUDA Mian)](https://i.ytimg.com/vi/SiBnK5DcWCU/hqdefault.jpg)

Source: Beyond Noodles
Tags: noodles, chinese, handcrafted, northwest
Time: 30 mins prep | 10 mins cook

## Ingredients

### Noodles (Method 1: Autolyse)

- 200g AP Flour
- 0.5 tsp Salt
- 100g Water
- 2 tbsp Oil

### Noodles (Method 2: Rolling Cut)

- 200g AP Flour
- 0.5 tsp Salt
- 100g Water
- 3 tbsp Oil

### Chili Oil Topping

- 3 in Sectional Leek, finely minced
- 2 cloves of Garlic, minced
- 4 tsp Chili Flakes
- 0.5 tsp Sichuan Peppercorn Powder, optional
- 4 tbsp Light Soy Sauce
- 1 tbsp Dark Chinese Vinegar
- 0.25 tsp Salt
- 5 tbsp Hot Oil

### Optional Vegetables

- Baby Bok Choy, Blanched for 45 seconds

## Method

1. For Method 1 (Autolyse): Mix flour and salt, slowly drizzle in water, knead for 1 minute. Cover and rest for 30 minutes. Knead for 2 minutes. Roll out and cut into four pieces.

   For Method 2 (Rolling Cut): Mix flour and salt, slowly drizzle in water, knead for 5 minutes. Roll out.

2. Pinch the sides of each dough piece and seal by pulling the dough across the work surface, letting the friction do the work (12 times).

3. Roll each piece into a log, coat in oil (2 tbsp for Method 1, 3 tbsp + 1 tbsp for Method 2), cover, and rest (2 hours for Method 1, 4-10 hours for Method 2).

4. Flatten a log with a rolling pin or hands. Press down the middle with a chopstick. Bang the noodle against the work surface (5 times). Pull the noodle apart from the center.

5. Boil noodles in rapidly boiling water until they float (about 1 minute).

6. Blanch optional vegetables (e.g., baby bok choy) if desired.

7. Combine topping ingredients: minced leek, garlic, chili flakes, Sichuan peppercorn powder, soy sauce, vinegar, and salt. Heat oil until it starts to smoke, pour over chili flakes.

8. Mix toppings, toss with noodles, and serve.

## Notes

Topping variations include tomatoes, neck stir-fry, Chinese chives, and stir-fried lamb with cumin.
