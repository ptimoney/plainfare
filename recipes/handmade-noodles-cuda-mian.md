# Handmade Noodles (CUDA Mian)

A recipe for handmade noodles, showcasing two methods: autolyse and rolling cut. The recipe features detailed instructions for the noodles themselves and a classic chili oil topping which is easily customizable.

## Ingredients

### Noodles (Autolyse Method)

- 200g AP Flour
- 0.5 tsp Salt
- 100g Water
- 2 tbsp Oil

### Noodles (Rolling Cut Method)

- 200g AP Flour
- 0.5 tsp Salt
- 100g Water
- 3 tbsp Oil

### Chili Oil Topping

- Finely Minced Leek, 3-inch sectional
- 2 cloves of Minced Garlic
- 4 tsp Chili Flakes
- 0.5 tsp Sichuan Peppercorn Powder, optional
- 4 tbsp Light Soy Sauce
- 1 tbsp Dark Chinese Vinegar
- 0.25 tsp Salt
- 5 tbsp Hot Oil

### Vegetable

- Baby Bok Choy

## Method

1. Combine AP flour and salt. Slowly drizzle in water, mixing until it just comes together (autolyse method).

2. Cover and rest for 30 minutes.

3. Knead for about 2 minutes to get a nice smooth dough.

4. Pinch together the craggily uneven side to shape the dough.

5. Pull the dough across the work surface 12 times to seal, letting friction do the work.

6. Roll out the dough and cut into four pieces.

7. Pinch where cut, roll out to roughly 10-inch logs.

8. Coat each log thoroughly in oil.

9. Cover and rest for 2 hours (for autolyse method).

10. Combine AP flour, salt and water. Knead for about 5 minutes (for rolling cut method).

11. Pinch and seal.

12. Oil with 2 tablespoons plus 1 extra tablespoon, thoroughly coating dough, and cover and rest for 4 to 10 hours.

13. Flatten the log with a rolling pin/hands.

14. Lightly press down the middle with a chopstick.

15. Smack against work surface about 5 times to lengthen.

16. Pull noodle apart at center.

17. Boil noodles in rapidly boiling water for about 1 minute until they float.

18. Cut the dough in half (if not using the entire space). Cut into 2-3 inch slices, then cut each slice in half.

19. Press down with a chopstick, pull apart, and boil.

20. Blanch baby bok choy for 45 seconds.

21. Combine leek, garlic, chili flakes, Sichuan peppercorn powder, soy sauce, dark Chinese vinegar and salt.

22. Heat oil until just starting to smoke, pour over chili flakes, and mix.

23. Toss noodles in boiling water, add cooked vegetables and chili sauce. Serve.

## Notes

Toppings can vary widely; other popular choices include tomatoes, neck stir-fry, Chinese chives, and stir-fried lamb with cumin. For best results, roll out the entire dough if possible.
